01 / Brand essence

Steady hands.
Soft voice.

Arham (अरहम) means peaceful, non-violent. The brand is calm under pressure — the visual equivalent of a paramedic who has done this a thousand times.

01

Care, captured

Every entry — a feed, a fluid, a photo — is a small act of accountability. The product makes it light, never bureaucratic.

02

Clinical, not cold

Information dense, but warm at the edges. Soft corners, generous spacing, no harsh shadows.

03

One screen, one task

Designed for a busy browser tab on a shared laptop. Sidebar pinned, primary action one click away, a forgiving keyboard.

Brand promise

We keep the record so you can keep the patient.

07 / Imagery

Real animals, real wards.

Imagery is documentary, never stock. Honest light, neutral white-balance, no over-cropping. Captions carry the clinical meaning — never the image alone.

on-admission · roadside
Bruno · 08:12RTA
x-ray · femur AP
Bruno · 10:05Dr. Mehta
post-op · wing pin
Pigeon · 15:30OT

Do

  • Photograph the animal where care actually happens — kennel, OT, stall.
  • Caption every image with patient, time, and context.
  • Use the four media kinds: photo · xray · video · doc.
  • Crop tight on the clinical detail; leave the rest honest.

Don't

  • Don't filter, sharpen or boost saturation on clinical photos.
  • Don't show identifiable owners or staff without consent on file.
  • Don't crop blood or wounds for "polish" — they are the record.
  • Don't use stock animals to represent real patients.
08 / Voice

Specific, short, kind.

We write the way a senior nurse hands off at shift change: facts first, no decoration, never alarming. Numbers belong in numerals. Prefer verbs.

tone

Steady

Even at "Critical", copy never panics. Severity is signaled by status color and ordering, not by exclamation marks.

tense

Present-active

"Fluids running" beats "Fluids have been administered". Active verbs save time and weight.

number

Numerals always

"5 mg", "3 days", "44 kg". Words for counted things slow the scan.

Round note · do✓ approved
Temp 102.4. Pain 6/10. Mild dehydration. Continue fluids, recheck in 4 h.
Round note · don't✕ rewrite
Patient appears to be experiencing some elevated temperature and possibly some discomfort — please consider reassessing at a later point in time!!
Empty state · do✓ approved
No rounds yet today. Tap + to log the first one.
Empty state · don't✕ rewrite
Oops! Looks like there's nothing here yet 🐾 Why not be the first to add something?
Alert · do✓ approved
Laxmi — no update in 6 h. Last note: Sahil, 11:00 walk.
Alert · don't✕ rewrite
⚠️ URGENT! This patient may need immediate attention from your team right away!!

Glossary & conventions

Always

IPD · OT · ICU · RTA · post-op · sono · CBC · IM · IV · PO

Animal pronouns

Use the patient's name, then "he" or "she". Never "it".

Times

12-hour with AM/PM in UI; 24-hour in exports. "Just now", "4 min ago" up to 1 h.

09 / Accessibility

Wet glove, bright sun, cracked screen.

The app is used outdoors, one-handed, often on shared devices. Contrast and target size are non-negotiable; novelty is.

Contrast

AA minimum, AAA preferred

Body text passes ≥ 4.5:1. Status pills pass ≥ 4.5:1 in both light and Utility dark variants. Accent on white passes AA Large; never use accent for body text.

Target size

32 px min icon, 40 px primary

Icon-only buttons ≥ 32 × 32. Primary actions ≥ 40 px tall. Adjacent click targets must have ≥ 4 px gap. Nav items in the sidebar are 36 px tall.

Motion

Functional, not festive

Sheet rises 180 ms ease-out; row inserts crossfade 120 ms. Honor prefers-reduced-motion by removing transforms and shortening fades to 60 ms.

Status

Never color alone

Every status pill carries text plus a colored dot — readable for color-blind users and at a thumbnail glance.

Density check

Every primary flow must complete on a 1280 × 800 laptop with the browser at default zoom. Test the sidebar collapsed view next — reception staff regularly run the app docked beside a CCTV pane.
